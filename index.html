What is “digital ID” in cards?

Digital identity is the combination of data, documents, devices and signals that lets a card issuer know that you are really you – online, on mobile and at the point of sale.

  • • Verified personal data (name, address, date of birth)
  • • Official ID documents and selfies
  • • National eID or bank ID schemes
  • • Linked devices and trusted browsers
  • • Behaviour and usage history over time

KYC: Knowing who gets the card

“Know Your Customer” (KYC) checks are required before a card can be issued or limits increased:

  • • Document capture (passport, ID card, driver’s license)
  • • Liveness and selfie comparison
  • • Sanctions and PEP screening
  • • Address checks and bureau data
  • • Risk-based additional questions or proofs

Digital ID in Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

Factor type Example in card world
Something you know PIN, password, passphrase.
Something you have Phone as secure device, hardware token, card itself.
Something you are Fingerprint, face ID, voice or behaviour.

Digital ID ties these factors together into a consistent profile used by the issuer to approve logins, payments and sensitive actions.

Device binding & trusted environments

Modern card programs strongly link your identity to your devices:

  • • Registration of primary phone and sometimes secondary devices
  • • Secure elements or trusted execution environments on-device
  • • Browser and device fingerprinting for risk scoring
  • • Push-based approvals instead of SMS codes
  • • Easy revocation when a device is lost or sold

Why digital ID matters for cardholders

  • • Lower fraud and account takeover risk
  • • Faster approvals and smoother onboarding
  • • Less friction for low-risk payments
  • • Ability to unlock higher limits and more features
  • • Compliance with PSD2, AML and local regulations
